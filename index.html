Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 8, 2026

Ad Astra Kids (“we,” “our,” or “us”) is committed to protecting the privacy and safety of children who use our games and applications.

Our Services are designed for children and families and are intended to comply with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (“COPPA”), platform policies, and applicable privacy regulations.

1. Information We Collect

We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children, including:

  • Names
  • Email addresses
  • Phone numbers
  • Home addresses
  • Photographs
  • Voice recordings
  • Precise location information

Our applications do not include user accounts, chat systems, or social networking functionality.

2. Technical Information

We may collect limited technical information necessary for app functionality, analytics, performance monitoring, crash reporting, and security purposes.

This may include:

  • Device type
  • Operating system version
  • App version
  • General gameplay analytics
  • Crash diagnostics

3. Analytics

We use Firebase Analytics to understand app performance and improve user experience.

We configure our applications to support child-directed treatment where applicable.

4. Advertising

Our Services may display contextual non-personalized advertisements suitable for child-directed audiences.

We currently use:

  • LevelPlay Mediation
  • Unity Ads

Additional child-directed advertising providers may be added in future updates.

We do not permit personalized behavioral advertising in our child-directed applications.

5. Parental Gate

External links, in-app purchases, and parent-related sections may be protected behind a parental gate intended to prevent unintended child access.

6. Data Sharing

We do not sell personal information.

Limited technical data may be shared with trusted service providers strictly for:

  • Analytics
  • Advertising delivery
  • App functionality
  • Security and fraud prevention

7. Children's Privacy

Protecting children’s privacy is extremely important to us.

If a parent or guardian believes that a child has provided personal information to us, they may contact us and we will take reasonable steps to investigate and remove such information if necessary.

8. Updates To This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect operational, legal, or technical changes.
